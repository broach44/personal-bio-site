Background

In April 2011, I began working at CoreCivic (previously known as Corrections Corporation of America) as a coordinator in Operations Support department. I offered support for the financial business operations and occupational safety operations within the company. A few years later, I was promoted to oversee the Occupational Safety for all our Community Branch consisting of around 27 facilities and climbing. I am responsible for provideing guidance to these facilities in order to ensure their compliance with federal, state and local safety, health and environmental regulations. I have led multiple leadership projects that furthered the operational goals for integrating community facilities into the current business portfolio and fostering a culture of communication and collaboration.

Journey into Development

While I was working on finishing up my degree I took some brief certification courses with New Horizons. I completed their Microsoft Office certification program and the A+ certification program. Their program also allowed for you to add on an additional certification program. My program of choice was to learn Miscrosoft's SQL. I absolutely enjoyed this course but unfortunately due to life circumstances I was unable to continue down this path and gave it up before I was able to complete.

I am a person who loves to learn and I truly never want to stop learning new things that I find interesting. Because of this I decided to start developing. I felt (and currently still feel) very stale in my current role and was looking for something that would exercise and challenge my mind. I found this with coding. I spoke with one of my friends and listened to him talking about his role and projects and it was fascinating. He recommended Nashville Software School. He also recommended that I could start trying it out through Udemy and Team Treehouse sites. I immediately did all of those things and fell head over heels for software development. I have found my passion and feel I am made to do this. When coding it does not feel like workbut an enjoyable puzzle to be solved. There will always be one more puzzle to be solved so I look forward to all of those in the future!
